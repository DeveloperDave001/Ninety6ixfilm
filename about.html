Chapter 01

Born From Obsession

Two cameras. One rented warehouse. An unshakeable conviction that cinema could be more than entertainment — it could be truth.

First production set · Lagos, 2011

In 2011, David Inioluwa converted a disused printing factory in Surulere, Lagos into a working film studio. He had no investors, no distribution deals, and no industry connections. What he had was an archive of 4,000 films watched, an intimate understanding of African storytelling, and a refusal to make anything mediocre.

"The industry told us our stories weren't 'commercially viable.' We built a studio to prove that art and commerce aren't enemies — they're the same thing when the work is extraordinary."

— David Inioluwa

His debut short, Ekó at Dusk, screened at three international festivals in its first year. Shot on a borrowed 16mm Bolex with a crew of seven, it announced a new cinematic voice — one rooted in Lagos but speaking to the world. Within two years, Ninety6ixfilmco. Motion Pictures had its first feature commission from a UK broadcaster.

The studio grew not by chasing trends, but by obsessively protecting its creative vision. Every hire was made for passion first. Every project was chosen because it needed to exist — not because it was safe. Today, that same philosophy governs a studio with offices on three continents and a catalogue spanning 127 productions.

From the gritty urban realism of The Forgotten Quarter to the sweeping desert epic Shadows of Tomorrow, each film carries the same fingerprint — an unapologetic commitment to truth, craft, and the kind of imagery that refuses to be forgotten.

Our Journey

Fifteen Years.
One Direction.

2011
Founded in Lagos, Nigeria
David Inioluwa establishes the studio in a converted factory in Surulere. First production: the short film Ekó at Dusk, shot on 16mm Bolex.
Origin
2013
First International Recognition
Ekó at Dusk premieres at Sundance and FESPACO. The studio secures its first broadcast commission from Channel 4 UK for a feature documentary.
Milestone
2015
The Forgotten Quarter — Theatrical Release
Ninety6ixfilmco.'s debut feature film opens in 14 countries. Critics call it "the most vital urban African cinema since City of God." The film wins 7 international awards.
Landmark
2017
London Studio Opens
Second headquarters established in Shoreditch, London. The studio expands into commercial work and music video production, partnering with Mercury Prize artists and major luxury brands.
Expansion
2019
The Reel Talk Podcast Launches
Our long-form audio series on the craft of filmmaking reaches 2 million downloads in its first year. Guests include Oscar-winning DPs, studio executives, and emerging voices from the African diaspora.
Media
2021
Streaming Partnership & 10th Anniversary
Multi-year deal signed with a major streaming platform. The back catalogue becomes available globally. Studio celebrates a decade with a retrospective at the BFI Southbank.
Partnership
2023
New York Office & Shadows of Tomorrow
Third global office opens in Brooklyn. Shadows of Tomorrow becomes the studio's most ambitious production — shot entirely on 65mm in Morocco and Iceland, entering post-production in 2024.
